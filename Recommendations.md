# Recommendations

Based on the root cause analysis, the following recommendations were proposed and implemented to improve 
Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) scores and overall customer experience.

---

# 1. Addressing High AHT (Average Handling Time)

### Recommendation 1.1 - Strict Action on Behaviour Challenges

Associates found roaming around the floor on productive aux, engaging in non-work-related conversations on productive aux, 
or deliberately delaying customer responses were subjected to performance management actions, including documented warnings where required.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved productivity.
* Reduced Average Handling Time (AHT).
* Improved customer response times.

---

### Recommendation 1.2 - Supervisor Monitoring and Coaching

Supervisors were required to conduct regular live call/chat monitoring and provide immediate coaching wherever performance gaps were identified.

Additionally, all DSATs were required to be audited as soon as they became available in the customer feedback reports. Supervisors were expected to review the customer interaction, identify the root cause of the negative feedback, and conduct targeted coaching sessions with the associate to prevent similar occurrences in future interactions.

Coaching discussions focused on areas such as customer handling, communication skills, process adherence, ownership, knowledge gaps, transfer behaviour, and overall customer experience.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved customer handling.
* Faster issue resolution.
* Reduced repeat mistakes.
* Improved associate performance.
* Improved CSAT performance.
* Increased accountability through timely coaching interventions.

---

### Recommendation 1.3 - Mentor-Mentee Program

A structured mentor-mentee program was introduced by pairing Top Quartile (TQ) associates with Bottom Quartile (BQ) 
associates and Middle Quartile (MQ) associates with BQ associates, sitting arrangements to be prepared WFM team accordingly
as per the shift timing and system availibility of the associates.

### Expected Outcome

* Faster knowledge transfer.
* Improved associate performance.
* Reduced dependency on SMEs and supervisors.

---

# 2. Addressing Knowledge Gaps

### Recommendation 2.1 - Mandatory Utilization of Knowledge Resources

All associates were required to open and utilize Knowledge Base (KB) articles and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) 
while handling customer interactions.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved process adherence.
* Reduced incorrect resolutions.
* Reduced transfers.

---

### Recommendation 2.2 - Weekly Knowledge Refresh Sessions

Regular refresher sessions were conducted to reinforce product knowledge, process updates, and best practices.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved confidence in handling customer queries.
* Reduced knowledge-related errors.
* Improved First Contact Resolution (FCR).

---

# 3. Addressing Technical Issues

### Recommendation 3.1 - Dedicated IT Support

IT support personnel were made available to identify and resolve AWS Omnichannel tool issues impacting 
customer interactions.

### Expected Outcome

* Reduced chat and call disconnections.
* Improved customer experience.
* Reduced repeat contacts.

---

### Recommendation 3.2 - Client Escalation of Tool Issues

Tool-related issues were documented and escalated to client stakeholders along with supporting evidence and impact analysis.

### Expected Outcome

* Faster resolution of platform issues.
* Increased visibility of operational challenges.
* Improved system reliability.

---

# 4. Improving Communication Standards

### Recommendation 4.1 - Targeted Communication Coaching

Associates identified with accent-related challenges or communication gaps were enrolled in coaching sessions 
conducted by the Customer Experience and Learning & Development teams.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved communication quality.
* Better customer interactions.
* Higher customer satisfaction scores.

---

# 5. Reducing Repeat Contacts

### Recommendation 5.1 - Strengthen Transfer Processes

Knowledge Base articles and SOPs were updated to provide clear ownership and transfer guidelines.

Additionally, it was recommended to introduce a transfer-assist feature within AWS Omnichannel. 
Before transferring a customer interaction, associates would be required to select the issue category and sub-category 
(L1, L2, L3 classification). Based on the selections made, the system would automatically recommend the correct destination
queue or team for transfer.

To ensure compliance, completion of the L1, L2, and L3 fields should be made mandatory before a transfer can be initiated.

This enhancement would help associates identify the correct transfer path, especially in complex customer scenarios where 
ownership is unclear.

### Expected Outcome

* Reduced incorrect transfers.
* Improved First Contact Resolution (FCR).
* Reduced repeat contacts.
* Improved resolution accuracy.
* Reduced customer effort.
* Improved customer experience.
* Reduced dependency on SME intervention for transfer-related queries.

---

### Recommendation 5.2 - Escalation Management Framework

SMEs and Supervisors were required to actively support associates handling complex customer issues, especially in situations 
where an immediate resolution was not available.

For cases involving highly dissatisfied or irate customers, including customers threatening legal action, social media 
escalation, or formal complaints, SMEs and Supervisors were expected to intervene immediately and take ownership of 
the interaction.

Where required, the chat/call would be transferred to an SME or Supervisor to de-escalate the situation, 
provide assurance to the customer, and ensure appropriate follow-up until resolution.

Additionally, clear escalation paths and ownership guidelines were established to ensure timely resolution of complex 
customer concerns.

### Expected Outcome

* Faster resolutions.
* Reduced repeat contacts.
* Improved customer confidence.
* Better management of high-risk customer interactions.
* Reduced escalations to client stakeholders.
* Improved customer retention and brand perception.

---

# 6. Managing Complex Customer Queries

### Recommendation 6.1 - SME Ownership Model

For cases where immediate resolution was unavailable, SMEs were required to take ownership of the case, provide 
estimated turnaround times, and proactively update customers until resolution.

### Expected Outcome

* Improved customer trust.
* Reduced dissatisfaction.
* Better case management.

---

# 7. Workforce Coordination

### Recommendation 7.1 - Session Planning with WFM

Supervisors and SMEs coordinated with Workforce Management (WFM) teams to schedule coaching and knowledge sessions 
during periods of lower contact volume.

### Expected Outcome

* Minimal operational impact.
* Improved participation in development activities.
* Better workforce utilization.

---

# Expected Business Impact

Implementation of these recommendations was expected to:

* Improve CSAT scores.
* Reduce Average Handling Time (AHT).
* Reduce repeat contacts.
* Reduce incorrect transfers.
* Improve customer experience.
* Improve associate performance.
* Strengthen operational efficiency.

These recommendations were implemented through collaboration between Operations, Quality, Training, Workforce Management, 
SMEs, and Client Stakeholders.
