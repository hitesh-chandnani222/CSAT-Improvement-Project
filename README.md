# Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Improvement Project

## Project Overview

This Business Analysis case study focuses on improving Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) within an international customer support operation through root cause analysis, KPI monitoring, stakeholder collaboration, and process improvement initiatives.

## Business Problem

Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) scores were consistently below target, leading to increased customer dissatisfaction, higher escalations, and potential risk to client satisfaction.

## Objective

Improve CSAT performance from 82% to 89% while maintaining operational KPIs such as Average Handling Time (AHT), Transfer Rate, and Compliance.

## Stakeholders

* Operations Manager 
* Operations Supervisors
* Customer Support Associates
* Quality Team
* Training Team
* Workforce Management Team
* SMEs (Subject Matter Experts)
* Client Stakeholders

## Data Sources Analyzed

* CSAT Survey Results and Voice of Customer (VOC)
* Average Handling Time (AHT)
* Transfer Rate
* Repeat Contact Rate
* Quality Audit Reports
* Call and Chat Recordings
* Case Management Reports

## Key Findings

Analysis identified multiple drivers of customer dissatisfaction, including high handling times, knowledge gaps, tool-related disconnections, inconsistent communication standards, repeat contacts, and limited utilization of knowledge resources.

## Recommendations

* Implement targeted coaching programs
* Strengthen knowledge management practices
* Improve transfer handling procedures
* Introduce quality calibration sessions
* Enhance escalation management processes
* Improve monitoring and performance governance

## Results

* CSAT improved by 7%
* Transfer rates reduced
* Escalation rates decreased
* Customer experience improved
* Tool-related disconnection issues resolved
* Operational efficiency maintained
